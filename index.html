Most analysts learn data quality from a textbook. I learned it from the queue — reconciling patient, provider and appointment records at MediBuddy, where a wrong field meant a missed appointment, not just a broken chart.

That operational grounding now shapes how I analyze data: I don't just clean and visualize it, I ask what decision it's meant to support and whether it would survive contact with the real process behind it. Alongside my B.Tech in Computer Science, I completed a focused Data Analytics program covering Python, SQL, Power BI, Tableau, Machine Learning and Statistics — sharpening the analytical layer on top of that operations instinct.

Today I build interactive dashboards, automate reporting, and run exploratory analysis across domains as different as hospital logistics, city traffic, and factory floors — with one constant: insights a business can actually act on.
